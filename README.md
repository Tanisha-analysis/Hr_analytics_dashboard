# Attrition Analysis
![Attrition Analysis][hr_analytics_dashboards.png]

### 📌 One-Line Summary
Interactive HR dashboard analyzing 1470 employees to identify attrition drivers.  
Solved: Which department, age group, job role, education level has highest attrition so HR can take retention action.

---

### 🎯 Business Problem
Company facing 16.12% attrition rate = 237 employees left.  
**Questions answered:** 
1. Which department loses most people? R&D vs Sales vs HR
2. Which age group 25-34 vs 35-44 quits most? 
3. Which job roles are highest risk? Sales Representative 39.76%
4. Does travel frequency or education impact attrition?

---

### 📊 Key Insights from Dashboard
| Insight | Business Impact |
| --- | --- |
| **R&D = 56.12% of attrition** vs HR only 5.06% | R&D retention strategy needed urgently. Highest cost center |
| **Age 25-34 = 337F + 217M = 554 employees** highest headcount | Early career attrition risk. Engagement programs needed |
| **Sales Representative = 39.76% attrition rate** | Role redesign or incentive change required |
| **Life Sciences education = 89 attritions** highest | Hiring profile mismatch or role fit issue |
| **Active employees = 1233 vs Total 1470** | 237 attrition = direct revenue loss + hiring cost |

**KPIs Delivered:** Total Employees: 1470 | Total Attrition: 237 | Attrition Rate: 16.12% | Avg Age: 37 | Avg Monthly Pay: 6503

7. **Job Role Risk**: Bar chart - Sales Rep 39.76% highest attrition rate
